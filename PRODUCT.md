# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three-tier role hierarchy within each electrical installation company:

- **Owner** — full access and edit rights across all business units, workers, financials, and settings. Creates and manages the entire company account.
- **Manager** — access and edit rights scoped to their assigned business unit. Oversees workers, sites, and operations within that unit.
- **Employees** — six levels (Junior, Junior with degree, Mid, Mid with degree, Senior, Senior with degree). Access is scoped to assigned work sites, assigned tasks, and their own timesheet. Cannot see company-wide data.

Account creation flow: every non-owner account is created via email invitation or invite link sent by the owner when adding a worker. The worker confirms the email and completes their account setup.

Primary context: managers and office staff work at desks; field employees access the app on phones from active work sites, often in poor lighting, sometimes with gloves.

## Product Purpose

ElectricVision Track is an operations management platform for electrical installation companies. It manages the full lifecycle: sites, workers, task assignments, timesheets, quotes, offers, contracts, invoices, purchases, and inventory — in one place, scoped by role.

Success means a field worker logs their timesheet in under 30 seconds and a manager closes an invoice without touching a spreadsheet.

## Brand Personality

Bold, fast, industrial. Confident and direct — the tool knows what it's for and gets out of the way. No softness, no enterprise bureaucracy.

## Anti-references

No explicit anti-references selected. Preserve the existing dark industrial aesthetic — the current dark theme and gold signature color are on-brand and should be maintained.

## Design Principles

1. **Role clarity first** — every screen communicates what this user can do. No dead ends, no silent permission failures, no UI that exists but doesn't work for a given role.
2. **Speed over depth** — data entry must be fast. Field workers on phones from a job site have five seconds of attention. Optimize for quick input, not comprehensive forms.
3. **Industrial confidence** — bold type, high contrast, dense information. No decorative softness. The UI should feel like a well-made tool, not a consumer app.
4. **Density with hierarchy** — electrical operations involve many parallel data streams. Pack information efficiently; use visual hierarchy to separate what's urgent from what's reference.
5. **Mobile-first field reality** — touch targets, contrast, and readability must hold up on a phone screen in outdoor daylight with one hand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High-contrast dark theme is the baseline. Touch targets minimum 44px for field (mobile) use. Support for both Romanian and English (current i18n setup).
